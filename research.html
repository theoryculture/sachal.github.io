Research

Graph Theory · Combinatorics · Discrete Mathematics

My research interests lie in graph theory and discrete mathematics, with a particular focus on structural properties of graphs, Hamiltonian cycles, and combinatorial constructions.

I am especially interested in problems related to the existence and characterization of unique Hamiltonian cycles, as well as structural conditions that guarantee rigidity or uniqueness in graph embeddings.

More broadly, I explore questions in extremal graph theory and combinatorics, often motivated by understanding how global properties of a graph arise from local constraints.

Current Focus

At present, I am working on problems related to Hamiltonicity and structural graph properties, including exploring new constructions and potential counterexamples in conjectural settings.

Open Problems of Interest

  • Conditions for the existence of unique Hamiltonian cycles
  • Structural graph properties that enforce rigidity or uniqueness
  • Extremal problems in graph theory
  • Constructive approaches to combinatorial conjectures

I am particularly drawn to problems that combine constructive techniques with deep structural insights, especially where new examples or counterexamples can shed light on conjectures.
