About

Who I Am

I'm a mathematician and computer scientist at the Department of Mathematics and Physics, Charles University, Prague, with a research focus in Graph Theory — specifically the existence and uniqueness of Hamiltonian cycles.

Previously at Forman Christian College, Lahore, where I worked on Game Theory and AI. I've lectured at the Czech MENSA Summer Camp, taught at the International School of Prague, and tutored university and high school students since 2018.

Exploring Sheehan's Conjecture: do uniquely Hamiltonian regular graphs exist? Extending Fleischner's construction via the Blanuša snark.

Outside research, I write about philosophy and the playful side of mathematics.
